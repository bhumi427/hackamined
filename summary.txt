The story "When the Trees Walked" is about a boy who spends time with his Grandfather on their porch. One day, he notices a vine moving towards his Grandfather, and he imagines it wants to be near him because Grandfather is a very kind person.

Grandfather loves trees and knows a lot about them from his years working in the Indian Forest Service. After he retired, he built a house and planted many trees around it. Although Grandmother likes flowers more, Grandfather helps her with gardening because it attracts butterflies.

Grandfather believes it's important to plant trees not just for people but also for animals and the environment. He worries that if people keep cutting down trees without planting new ones, the Earth could become a desert. He encourages the boy to help him plant trees in the forest, teaching him a poem about protecting trees.

Together, they find a dry spot by a river and plant saplings. The boy enjoys the beauty of nature, especially during the rainy season, when everything grows green. But later, the boy goes to a boarding school, and his grandparents move to England.

Years later, when the boy returns to visit, he finds the trees they planted are thriving and have even formed a small paradise. The trees seem to recognize him, and he feels happy knowing Grandfather's dream of having trees grow and flourish has come true.

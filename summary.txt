In the narrative "When the Trees Walked," the speaker shares cherished memories of time spent with their Grandfather, who had a deep passion for nature, especially trees. Set in the idyllic surroundings of Dehradun during a North Indian winter, the story begins with the speaker observing a vine moving toward Grandfather, symbolizing a connection to nature. 

Grandfather, a retired member of the Indian Forest Service, has cultivated a lush garden filled with diverse trees and flowers, while Grandmother prefers nurturing flowers. This dynamic illustrates a balance between their differing gardening interests. Grandfather emphasizes the importance of trees, not just for beauty, but for ecological reasons, like preventing desertification and providing habitats for wildlife.

He instills a sense of responsibility in the speaker, stressing that planting trees is essential for the environment and future generations. They embark on tree-planting ventures in the nearby jungle, championing the idea that trees once roamed freely but were magically rooted in place—an idea Grandfather playfully presents. 

The narrative reflects on nature’s resilience, contrasting the lushness of the planted trees during the rainy season with the looming threat of concrete expansion. A significant turning point occurs when war separates the speaker from their home and Grandparents, leading to years spent away. 

Upon returning, the speaker finds a once-dry riverbed transformed into a green paradise, with the trees they planted thriving and harboring wildlife. The story concludes with a hopeful reflection on the enduring impact of their efforts, showing that, in a small corner of the world, Grandfather’s dream of revitalizing nature has come to fruition. 

The key takeaways include the importance of tree conservation, the relationship between humans and nature, the legacy of planting trees, and the joy of reconnecting with the environment. This narrative serves as a reminder that caring for our planet can lead to beautiful transformations.

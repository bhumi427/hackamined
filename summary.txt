Grandfather, a retired member of the Indian Forest Service, has instilled in the narrator a reverence for trees and the natural world, illustrated by the lush variety of flora he has lovingly planted around their home in Dehradun. The text contrasts Grandfather's passion for trees with Grandmother’s inclination toward flowers, demonstrating a harmonious coexistence of differing values in gardening. This relationship is emblematic of the family's appreciation for nature, with Grandfather’s practical approach fueled by a desire to attract butterflies and support local ecosystems.

Grandfather’s advocacy for tree planting extends beyond personal enjoyment; he emphasizes the ecological imperative of restoring forests for wildlife and combating environmental degradation. His concerns about deforestation and its consequences are articulated through a prophetic vision of a future devoid of trees, motivating the narrator’s active participation in planting initiatives. The rituals of planting trees in an abandoned forest space are punctuated by lessons about environmental stewardship, underscored by the reading of a poignant poem that reflects a commitment to conservation.

The story also captures the organic interplay between human activities and the wild, showcasing how nature continually asserts itself, as seen in the remarkable growth of flora in unexpected places, which presents an enchanting challenge for domestic life. This tension culminates in the backdrop of World War II, marking a transitional period as the narrator moves away for education, whilst temporal changes in the landscape echo the shifts in family dynamics.

Years later, upon returning to Dehradun, the narrator experiences a poignant reconnection with that ecology as he discovers the vibrant growth and thriving ecosystems nurtured through his and Grandfather's earlier efforts. The once-dry riverbed now showcases a "small green paradise," symbolizing resilience and the fruition of their shared vision for nature's revival. The presence of animals, such as squirrels and a koel bird, reinforces the idea of a flourishing habitat that celebrates the legacy of tree-planting and the deeper connection fostered between humans and the living world.

In summary, "When the Trees Walked" is a reflective narrative exploring the interaction between individuals and their environment, highlighting the significance of sustainability, familial connections, and the profound impact one can have on nature. Through Grandfather's wisdom and the narrator's journey, the tale illuminates how the aspirations for ecological balance and beauty can be realized, ultimately portraying a narrative of hope and continuity within the sphere of the natural world.

**Summary of "When the Trees Walked":**

The narrative begins with a reflective moment shared between the speaker and their Grandfather on a serene winter morning in North India. The speaker observes a creeping vine's tender movement towards Grandfather's feet, leading to a deeper appreciation of nature's quiet presence. Grandfather, a former member of the Indian Forest Service, cultivated a lush garden of diverse trees and plants around their home in Dehradun, including fruit trees and ornamental varieties. 

While Grandfather loved trees for their beauty and ecological significance, Grandmother preferred flowers and busily tended to her garden to attract butterflies. Together, they balanced their interests, as Grandfather believed in nurturing the environment, planting trees not only for aesthetics but for wildlife and the health of the ecosystem. He passionately explained the crucial role trees play in combating desertification, attracting rainfall, and providing resources for humans and wildlife.

The story transitions to a whimsical idea proposed by Grandfather—that trees once had the ability to walk, but were enchanted to remain still. He emphasizes the importance of planting trees during their treks into the forest, highlighting the inevitability of deforestation if proactive measures aren’t taken. Their shared experiences cultivating saplings, particularly on an island in a riverbed, forged a bond between the two.

As the narrative continues, the speaker transitions to life changes brought on by World War II, including boarding school and moving away from India. Years later, upon returning to Dehradun, the speaker finds the landscape transformed, with the trees they once planted blossoming vibrantly and providing a rich habitat for wildlife. The sight of the thriving flora symbolically demonstrates that Grandfather's vision for a verdant, flourishing environment has come to fruition, as if the trees themselves are moving once again.

Overall, the document celebrates the importance of trees and environmental stewardship, the bonds formed between generations through nature, and the enduring impacts of nurturing the land.
